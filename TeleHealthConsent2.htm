TeleHealth

BETH ISRAEL DEACONESS MEDICAL CENTER (BIDMC) AND HARVARD MEDICAL FACULTY PHYISICIANS AT BETH ISRAEL DEACONESS MEDICAL CENTER (HMFP)

CONSENT TO PARTICIPATE IN A TELEMEDICINE VISIT

1. I hereby consent to engage in a telemedicine visit with a provider of BIDMC or HMFP (the “Practice”) as part of my continuing care and treatment. The services delivered by the provider during this telemedicine visit are limited to evaluation and management services.

2. What is telemedicine? I understand that telemedicine uses interactive two-way audio-video conferencing technologies between a provider and a patient who are not in the same physical location and, as such, the services may not be as complete as a face-to-face visit with my provider. The alternative to a telemedicine visit is a face-to-face visit with a Practice provider.

3. Privacy and Security in Telemedicine. I understand that the laws regarding patient access to medical records and those that protect the confidentiality of my medical information also apply to telemedicine visits. The interactive technologies used in telemedicine incorporate network and software security protocols to protect the confidentiality of patient information transmitted. These protocols include measures to safeguard the data and to aid in protecting against intentional or unintentional corruption.

4. Participants. I understand that I will be informed of the identities of all parties present during the telemedicine visit and have the right to ask any individuals to leave the remote provider’s location. As part of the teaching mission of this Hospital, I understand that physicians in training (known as “residents”) may also be involved in my telehealth visits.

5. Risks involved in using Telemedicine Equipment. The potential risks of telemedicine are that the privacy and security of transmitting health information cannot be guaranteed and that electronic exchanges may experience errors or delays. In the case of technology or equipment failure, the Practice will arrange for a face-to-face visit with a provider.

6. Benefits of Telemedicine. No Assurances on Outcome. The benefits of telemedicine include convenient, real-time access to Practice providers. While I understand that I may benefit from telemedicine, I also understand that the results cannot be guaranteed or assured.

7. Right To Decline Services. I understand that I may decline to receive services from the Practice via telemedicine at any time without jeopardizing my access to future care, services, and benefits.

8. Financial Consent for Telemedicine Billing. I understand that I may receive a bill for services rendered via telemedicine and I that I might also be responsible for any applicable co-payment and/or deductible for my visit depending upon the nature of any insurance coverage I maintain.

9. I have read this document carefully and understand the risks and benefits of receiving my care via telemedicine. I understand that I may receive a bill for this telemedicine visit and may also have copayment or deductible obligations. I have had my questions regarding the visit explained. By proceeding with this telemedicine visit, I hereby consent to telemedicine treatment with the remote Practice provider under the terms described herein.
